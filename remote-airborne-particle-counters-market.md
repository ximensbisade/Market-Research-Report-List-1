<p><ul><li>Ionising</li><li>Non-ionising</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The remote airborne particle counters market is divided into two primary types: ionizing and non-ionizing. </p><p>Ionizing particle counters utilize radiation to detect and measure particles in the air, offering precise results even in challenging environments. In contrast, non-ionizing particle counters rely on light scattering or other non-invasive methods to quantify airborne particles. While ionizing counters are suitable for high-accuracy applications, non-ionizing options are favored for their ease of use and safety, catering to diverse industries such as pharmaceuticals, cleanrooms, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
