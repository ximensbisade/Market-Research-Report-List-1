<p><p>The aviation warning lights market is categorized into three main types: low-intensity, medium-intensity, and high-intensity warning lights. Low-intensity lighting is typically used for structures below 45 meters, providing visibility without causing significant light pollution. Medium-intensity lights are utilized for taller structures between 45 to 150 meters, ensuring aircraft safety while balancing energy efficiency. High-intensity lights serve towers exceeding 150 meters, emitting powerful signals visible from great distances, crucial for preventing collisions in busy flight paths.</p></p>
