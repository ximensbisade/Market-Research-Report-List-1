<p><p>The prosthetic sockets market encompasses various types, primarily categorized into transfemoral and transtibial (leg) sockets. Transfemoral sockets are designed for individuals with above-knee amputations, providing stability and support while enabling mobility. They typically feature advanced materials and customization for comfort and fit. Transtibial sockets, meant for below-knee amputations, focus on optimizing alignment and weight distribution, enhancing functionality. Both socket types are crucial for integrating prosthetic limbs, improving quality of life, and promoting independence for amputees.</p></p>
