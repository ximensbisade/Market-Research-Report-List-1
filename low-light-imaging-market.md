<p><p>The low-light imaging market primarily features two sensor technologies: CMOS and CCD. CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) sensors are known for their energy efficiency, faster readout speeds, and compact designs, making them suitable for a variety of applications. In contrast, CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) sensors excel in image quality and low noise performance, particularly in high sensitivity scenarios. Both technologies cater to different segments, such as security, automotive, and medical imaging, with evolving innovations enhancing their capabilities for low-light conditions.</p></p>
